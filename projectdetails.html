Problem Space and How It Was Arrived At

Ohara: City of Dreams aims to resolve key urban challenges by integrating efficient traffic management and sustainable development solutions. The project addresses critical issues such as congestion, emergency routing, smart parking, and public transport scheduling to create a highly functional urban environment.

Traffic congestion and inefficient public transit were identified as major barriers to smooth urban living. The project prioritizes optimizing vehicle flow, emergency response times, and sustainable transportation systems.

Findings from White Paper

1. Congestion Trends: Increasing traffic jams due to poor routing and signal management.

2. Public Transit Gaps: Inconsistent schedules causing underutilization of buses and trains.

3. Parking Issues: Time wasted searching for parking spots in busy areas.

4. Emergency Delays: Lack of effective routing for emergency vehicles.

5. Environmental Impact: High emissions from idling vehicles in congested zones.

Reflection on City Design

The city design incorporates the following principles:

  • Traffic Management: Implementing algorithms to monitor and reduce congestion in real time.
  • Emergency Vehicle Routing: Dynamic routing to ensure timely response for critical situations.
  • Sustainability: Creating eco-friendly urban spaces with parks and green zones.
  • Technology Integration: Leveraging AI and IoT for adaptive urban planning and traffic solutions.

Problem Definition

The Ohara project addresses urban challenges by applying advanced algorithms and technology to improve traffic management, emergency response, and sustainable city planning. The vision is to create a seamless and efficient urban environment where innovation meets livability.
